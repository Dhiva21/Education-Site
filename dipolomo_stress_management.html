The "Stress Management" diploma course is typically beneficial for individuals who want to improve their ability to handle workload efficiently and reduce stress levels. Here are some groups of people who could benefit from attending such a course:

  1. Professionals: Anyone in a demanding job where time management is crucial, such as managers, executives, entrepreneurs, or project leaders.
  2. Students: Especially those facing heavy workloads, exams, and deadlines.
  3. Parents: Juggling between family responsibilities and personal time can be stressful, making time management essential.
  4. Freelancers and Remote Workers: Managing one's own schedule and workload effectively is critical for success.
  5. Anyone experiencing stress: People who feel overwhelmed by their responsibilities and need strategies to cope better.
  6. Career Changers: Individuals transitioning into new roles or industries that require different time management skills.
  7. Small Business Owners: Managing multiple aspects of a business can be stressful, making effective time management crucial.
  8. Healthcare Professionals: Managing time efficiently is critical in healthcare settings where patient care and administrative tasks must be balanced.
  9. Educators: Teachers and professors managing classroom time and administrative duties can benefit from effective time management strategies.
  10. Anyone interested in personal development: Learning to manage time and stress effectively contributes to overall well-being and productivity.

Ultimately, anyone looking to enhance their productivity, reduce stress, and improve their work-life balance can benefit from attending a "Time & Stress Management" diploma course

SRM Online Learning Portal: Remote Proctored Examination System (RPES) - Learner's Checklist

  1. Examination Mode: Remote Online Proctored Examination only (Theory/Practical/Project). No physical appearance is required.
  2. Online Examination Platform: SRM Online Remote Proctored Examination System.
  3. Mock test: It will be conducted before every end semester examination for software practice.
  4. End semester exam Pattern: Objective Type (MCQs) and Descriptive questions (Essay/Case study).
  5. For descriptive questions:  Students need to upload the scanned answer sheet in picture format (JPG) before the exam time gets over into the RPES.
  6. Examination Hardware/Software Requirements: Laptop or Desktop with webcam, Windows Operating System, Good internet bandwidth, Mobile or Scanner for scanning answer sheet(s), A4 sheets as per your requirement, pen (blue or black), pencil if required. 
  7. E-Answer sheet: Scanned answer sheet (handwritten in A4 sheets) should be uploaded before the exam time ends into the RPES.
  8. Authentication: SRMIST ID card and hall ticket / Admit card is mandatory to appear for the exam. 
  9. Back Paper(s): Learners can appear for Arrears / Back papers along with every next semester till the time of maximum number of attempts permitted as per regulation.
